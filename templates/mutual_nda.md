# MUTUAL NON-DISCLOSURE AGREEMENT

**This Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement** ("Agreement") is entered into as of [DATE] between:

**[PARTY A NAME]**, a [company type] incorporated in [jurisdiction], with its registered office at [address] ("**Party A**"); and

**[PARTY B NAME]**, a [company type] incorporated in [jurisdiction], with its registered office at [address] ("**Party B**")

(each a "**Party**" and together the "**Parties**").

---

## 1. PURPOSE

The Parties wish to explore a potential business relationship or transaction (the "**Purpose**"). In connection with the Purpose, each Party (as "**Disclosing Party**") may disclose certain confidential information to the other Party (as "**Receiving Party**"). This Agreement sets out the terms on which such information shall be received and protected.

---

## 2. DEFINITION OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

2.1 "**Confidential Information**" means any information, whether written, oral, electronic, or in any other form, disclosed by or on behalf of the Disclosing Party to the Receiving Party, whether before or after the date of this Agreement, that:

(a) is designated as "confidential" or "proprietary" at the time of disclosure; or

(b) by its nature or the circumstances of its disclosure ought reasonably to be treated as confidential.

2.2 Confidential Information includes, without limitation: business plans, financial data, customer lists, technical specifications, source code, trade secrets, pricing information, and any analyses, compilations, studies, or other materials prepared by the Receiving Party that contain or reflect any Confidential Information.

2.3 For oral disclosures, information shall constitute Confidential Information only if designated as confidential at the time of disclosure.

> **⚠ Trainable clause — see --learn mode:**
> Section 2.1's "whether before or after the date of this Agreement" is a broad temporal scope.
> Section 2.3 omits a written confirmation window for oral disclosures — a common gap.

---

## 3. OBLIGATIONS OF THE RECEIVING PARTY

3.1 The Receiving Party shall:

(a) hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence;

(b) not disclose any Confidential Information to any third party without the prior written consent of the Disclosing Party;

(c) use the Confidential Information solely for the Purpose and for no other purpose;

(d) apply at least the same degree of care to protect the Confidential Information as it applies to its own confidential information, and in any event no less than reasonable care; and

(e) promptly notify the Disclosing Party upon becoming aware of any actual or suspected breach of confidentiality.

---

## 4. STANDARD CARVEOUTS

4.1 The obligations in Clause 3 shall not apply to information that:

(a) is or becomes generally available to the public other than through a breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party;

(b) was rightfully in the possession of the Receiving Party prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party, without any obligation of confidentiality;

(c) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information; or

(d) is received from a third party who is not subject to any obligation of confidentiality with respect to such information.

---

## 5. PERMITTED DISCLOSEES

5.1 The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information to its directors, officers, and employees (and in the case of a corporate entity, those of its subsidiaries and affiliates) who:

(a) have a need to know such information for the Purpose; and

(b) are bound by obligations of confidentiality at least as protective as those set out in this Agreement.

5.2 The Receiving Party shall be responsible for any breach of this Agreement by any person to whom it discloses Confidential Information pursuant to this Clause 5.

> **⚠ Trainable clause:** Professional advisors (lawyers, accountants, financial advisors) are not
> expressly included as permitted disclosees. This is a common gap that a receiving party should address.

---

## 6. COMPELLED DISCLOSURE

6.1 Notwithstanding Clause 3, the Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information to the extent required by applicable law, court order, or the rules of any applicable regulatory authority or stock exchange, provided that the Receiving Party:

(a) gives the Disclosing Party prompt prior written notice (to the extent permitted by law) of the requirement to disclose;

(b) cooperates with and assists the Disclosing Party, at the Disclosing Party's reasonable cost and expense, to seek a protective order or other available remedy; and

(c) discloses only so much of the Confidential Information as is legally required.

---

## 7. RETURN AND DESTRUCTION OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

7.1 Upon written request by the Disclosing Party, or upon termination of this Agreement, the Receiving Party shall, with the Disclosing Party's written consent, either:

(a) promptly return to the Disclosing Party all materials containing Confidential Information; or

(b) destroy all such materials and certify such destruction to the Disclosing Party in writing within ten (10) business days.

7.2 Notwithstanding Clause 7.1, the Receiving Party shall not be required to return or destroy copies of Confidential Information held in automated backup systems, provided that such backup copies are not accessible in the normal course of business and are destroyed in the normal course of the Receiving Party's backup retention policy.

> **⚠ Trainable clause:**
> Section 7.1 gives destruction/return as alternatives only "with the Disclosing Party's written
> consent" — this removes the Receiving Party's unilateral right to elect destruction. Market
> standard is the Receiving Party's election.

---

## 8. TERM

8.1 This Agreement shall come into force on the date first written above and shall continue for a period of **three (3) years**, unless earlier terminated by either Party upon thirty (30) days' written notice to the other.

8.2 Notwithstanding termination or expiration of this Agreement, the confidentiality obligations set out herein shall survive and remain in force for a period of **three (3) years** from the date of termination or expiration.

> **⚠ Trainable clause:** Three-year confidentiality tail is longer than the two-year market
> standard many receiving parties prefer. The purpose and sensitivity of the information should
> guide the negotiated position.

---

## 9. GOVERNING LAW AND DISPUTE RESOLUTION

9.1 This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of [GOVERNING LAW JURISDICTION], without regard to its conflict of law principles.

9.2 Any dispute arising out of or in connection with this Agreement, including any question regarding its existence, validity, or termination, shall be finally resolved by arbitration administered by the [ARBITRAL INSTITUTION] under its rules, which are deemed incorporated by reference into this Clause 9.2.

9.3 The seat of arbitration shall be [SEAT OF ARBITRATION]. The language of arbitration shall be English. The arbitral tribunal shall consist of one (1) arbitrator.

---

## 10. GENERAL PROVISIONS

10.1 **Assignment.** Neither Party may assign or transfer its rights or obligations under this Agreement without the prior written consent of the other Party, which shall not be unreasonably withheld. Any purported assignment without such consent shall be void.

10.2 **Entire Agreement.** This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties with respect to its subject matter and supersedes all prior discussions, representations, and agreements relating to confidentiality between the Parties.

10.3 **Amendments.** No amendment to this Agreement shall be effective unless made in writing and signed by authorised representatives of both Parties.

10.4 **Severability.** If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid, illegal, or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

10.5 **No Waiver.** The failure of either Party to enforce any provision of this Agreement shall not constitute a waiver of that Party's right to enforce such provision in the future.

10.6 **Notices.** All notices under this Agreement shall be in writing and sent to the addresses set out at the beginning of this Agreement (or such other address as a Party notifies in writing).

10.7 **Counterparts.** This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original, and all of which together shall constitute one and the same agreement. Electronic signatures shall be deemed valid.

10.8 **No Licence.** Nothing in this Agreement grants any licence, right, title, or interest in any intellectual property rights of the Disclosing Party, except as necessary for the Purpose.

10.9 **Residuals.** Nothing in this Agreement prevents the Receiving Party from using general skills, knowledge, and experience retained in unaided memory that may be incidentally recalled from exposure to the Confidential Information, provided such use does not constitute a wilful memorisation of specific data points.
